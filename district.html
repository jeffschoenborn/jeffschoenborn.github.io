About the District

North and Northeast Baltimore

The 14th Legislative District covers a broad swath of North and Northeast Baltimore, encompassing some of the city's most distinctive and beloved neighborhoods. Jeff Schoenborn has lived in this district for years — currently in Guilford — and knows its streets, its residents, and its needs.

The district's neighborhoods range from historic rowhouse communities to parkside enclaves, and include precincts that span council districts 9, 12, and 13. That diversity is a strength — and a reminder that effective representation requires listening, not assumption.

Guilford

Jeff's home neighborhood. Tree-lined streets, historic architecture, and a strong civic tradition.

Waverly

A proud working-class neighborhood with deep roots and a strong sense of community identity.

Lake Montebello

Parkside living anchored by the reservoir — one of Baltimore's most walkable neighborhoods.

Clifton Park

Home to Clifton Park and its historic mansion, golf course, and some of the city's best green space.

Pen Lucy

A stable residential neighborhood where longtime homeowners form the backbone of civic life.

Coldstream–Homestead

Historic rowhouse streets with a deep community character and an active neighborhood association.

Jeff knows the 14th District not as a collection of precincts on a map, but as a set of real communities with real concerns — about safety on their streets, the condition of their parks, the quality of their schools, and whether the city's leaders are paying attention.

How the Central Committee Is Structured

The Baltimore City Republican Central Committee mirrors the structure of the Baltimore City Council. Voters in each of the city's council districts elect two Republican committee members to represent them — one of whom Jeff is seeking to be for the 14th District. This structure ensures that every part of the city has a direct voice in how the local Republican Party is organized and led.

Jeff's Position

Choosing the Right People to Serve

One of the Central Committee's most consequential responsibilities is filling vacancies in the Maryland General Assembly. When a seat opens, the committee appoints a replacement — and that decision matters. Jeff has a clear standard for who deserves that appointment, looking for the most qualified person to serve, not the most connected:

  • Someone who genuinely knows the community they want to represent — not just its map, but its people and their real concerns.
  • Someone with the wisdom to think through the second and third-order effects of legislation — to ask not just "does this seem compassionate?" but "what incentives does this change, and for whom?"
  • Someone with real-world experience — ideally in business, working with diverse people and competing interests.
  • A servant, not an egoist looking for the next rung on the ladder.
